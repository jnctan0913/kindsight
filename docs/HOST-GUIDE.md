# Kindsight Host Guide

For a first-time host running a 20 to 30 minute session with 8 to 30 people. Kindsight adapts the paper icebreaker "你背后的光" (The Light Behind You). The app runs the mechanics. You hold the room. This guide covers both.

---

## 1. Before the session (15 minutes)

**Room and AV checklist**

- [ ] Projector or big TV connected. Open `/screen/[code]` on it once your room exists. The big screen never leaks note content, so it is safe to show at all times.
- [ ] Wifi confirmed for the venue guest network. Every player needs it.
- [ ] Ask players to arrive with phones charged. Bring one spare charger.
- [ ] Music source ready: a soft instrumental playlist, no lyrics. You will play it during writing only.
- [ ] Your laptop plugged in. The room survives your laptop dying, but do not test that on purpose.

**Create the room**

- Pick your mode at creation. **Round-robin for 6 or more people.** It guarantees everyone an even wall and you never have to chase anyone. **Free select only for small groups or high-trust teams** that will write generously without assignment. Under 6 people the console warns you that rotation anonymity weakens; take the free-select recommendation.
- Round count: accept the suggested number. Timer: 3 minutes per round is right for most groups.
- Roster: paste your name list, one name per line. Fix duplicates now, not in the lobby.

**Rehearse once**

Run the console's "Preview the ritual" demo with fake notes. Feel the three-breath hold and the one-by-one reveal yourself. You cannot pace a room through a moment you have never experienced. Five minutes, once, before your first real session.

## 2. Run sheet: 25-minute session

### 0:00 to 3:00: Lobby

Big screen shows the QR and room code. Say "Scan, find your name, claim it." Watch the claim counter on your console. Chase stragglers by name. Do not start until everyone is in.

### 3:00 to 6:00: Briefing

Advance to Briefing. The rules appear on their phones, but the demo below is yours to perform. It lands harder than any validator.

> "You are going to write anonymous kind notes to each other. Three frames only: 'I noticed you...', 'I think you're strong at...', 'I hope you...'. Before you write anything, let me read you the two worst notes in the history of this game.
>
> Note one: 'nice person'. That is not a note. That is a fortune cookie with low effort.
>
> Note two: 'good luck'. Good luck with what? Crossing the road?
>
> Here is the fix. Instead of 'nice person': 'I noticed you stayed behind on Tuesday to help Mei repack all the kits when everyone else left.' Instead of 'good luck': 'I hope you get to lead the next client pitch, because you are more ready than you think.' Specific beats nice. Every time. Vague praise fades. Details last."

> 「你们要匿名给彼此写善意的便签。只有三个开头：'我注意到你...'、'我觉得你很擅长...'、'我希望你...'。动笔之前，先听我念这个游戏史上最差的两条：
>
> 第一条：'人很好'。这不是便签，这是没诚意的幸运饼干。
>
> 第二条：'祝你好运'。祝什么好运？过马路吗？
>
> 正确的写法是：不写'人很好'，写'我注意到你周二留下来帮 Mei 重新打包了所有物料，那时候大家都走了'。不写'祝你好运'，写'我希望下次客户提案由你来主讲，你比你以为的更有准备'。具体永远胜过客气。空话会淡，细节会留。」

Let them laugh at the bad examples. The laugh is the memory hook.

### 6:00 to 16:00: Writing

Advance to Writing. **Music on now.** Soft instrumental lowers self-consciousness; a silent room makes people write safe, boring notes.

- **Minutes 0 to 2 will be quiet.** People are finding their first target. Do not panic and do not fill the silence with talking.
- **Minutes 2 to 8 are the dense period.** Watch your console, not the room. In free select, the coverage view sorts thin walls to the top and flags anyone under the 3-note floor in amber. Nudge the room, never the person:

> "Quick scan of the roster. A few walls are still thin. If you have not written to someone yet, that is your next note."

- Skim the moderation feed as notes flow. You see content and target, never authors. Kill anything mean-edged on sight (see section 3).
- In round-robin, players who finish early can write one bonus note. Point this out to fast writers.
- **Call the last round firmly.** Soft endings kill momentum: "This is the last round. Finish the note you are on. Sixty seconds." When you advance, unfinished drafts get a 10-second grace countdown, then auto-send or discard. Do not extend. Ever.

### 16:00 to 17:00: Settle the room

**Music off. This is the line between game and ritual.** Wait for actual silence before you touch the Reveal button.

> "Phones face down on your lap for a moment. Look up. Everything on your phone right now was written for you by the people in this room. When I open the walls, hold the button and breathe with it. Three breaths. Then read slowly, one note at a time. We read in silence. No talking, no showing your neighbor, until I say so."

> 「先把手机扣在腿上，抬起头。此刻你手机里的每一句话，都是这个房间里的人写给你的。等我打开墙的时候，按住那个按钮，跟着它呼吸。三次。然后慢慢读，一条一条读。我们在安静中读。在我开口之前，不说话，也不给旁边的人看。」

Then press "Open the walls." Say nothing. Protect the silence with your whole body. If someone whispers, one raised hand, no words. This silence is the entire product.

### 17:00 to 21:00: Reveal

Watch the reveal status list (Holding / Reading / Finished). Players opt individual notes onto the big-screen highlight wall as they read; leave that toggle off for now. When most players show Finished, give the slow readers another 30 seconds. Never rush someone mid-wall.

### 21:00 to 24:00: Voluntary sharing

Exactly three questions, on the big screen via the prompt deck, one at a time. Hands only. **Never cold-call.** If no hand goes up after ten seconds, smile and move to the next question. If nobody shares at all, that is a valid outcome. Move on without comment.

> 1. "Which line made you pause?"
> 2. "Which line said something you never thought about yourself?"
> 3. "Which line do you wish you knew who wrote?"

> 1. 「哪一句让你停了下来？」
> 2. 「哪一句说出了一个你从没想过的自己？」
> 3. 「哪一句你最想知道是谁写的？」

### 24:00 to 25:00: Closing

Do not close with "great job everyone." Close with this, adapted from the original game's closing line:

> "What you take away today is not an image on a phone. It is the real, specific version of you that lives in other people's eyes. The version you never get to see on your own. Now you have."

> 「你今天带走的不是手机里的一张图。是活在别人眼里的那个真实、具体的你。那个你自己永远看不见的你。今天，你看见了。」

Pause two seconds. Then, lighter: "Save your walls before you leave."

## 3. When things go wrong

**A hurtful or off note appears.** Kill it from the moderation feed the moment you see it. The recipient never sees it and never learns a note was removed; no tombstone appears. Kill works in every phase, including after reveal. That is the app side. The human side: if you saw it before reveal, say nothing to anyone. If the recipient already read it, do not address it publicly. Find them privately after the session: "One note did not meet the spirit of the game and I removed it. That note was about the writer, not about you." Kill early and often; a killed borderline note costs nothing, a wounding note at reveal costs everything.

**Someone has a thin wall (free select).** The app will not let you open the walls until everyone has at least 3 notes, and the console names who is short. Never name them to the room. Nudge collectively: "Two more minutes. Check the roster; anyone you have not written to yet is your next note." The unwritten-to sort in the picker does the targeting for you.

**Someone refuses to participate.** Never force it. Make them a reader: they can write notes to others without revealing a wall of their own, or simply sit in during the reveal and sharing. Say "You can just write, or just watch. Both are fine." Forced vulnerability is the opposite of this game.

**A phone dies or will not connect.** Pair them with a neighbor: they dictate, the neighbor types into their own writing turns where possible, and at reveal they read the dead-phone player's wall together on one screen after the neighbor finishes their own. Imperfect, workable, keep moving.

**Your laptop dies.** The room lives on the server, not your machine. Reopen the host URL on any browser (borrow a laptop or use your phone) and the console restores at the current phase. Players notice nothing. Do not announce the failure.

**Someone cries at reveal.** It happens, and it is almost always moved-crying, not hurt-crying. Do not spotlight them, do not send the room's attention their way, do not ask "are you okay" across the room. Give them space and slow the pace slightly. If you are unsure which kind of crying it is, check in quietly one-on-one after sharing ends.

---

## 4. After the session

1. **Export reminder, twice.** Once at closing, once as people stand up: "Save your wall as an image now. When the room closes, the notes are gone. The image is the keepsake." The room also self-deletes after 24 hours, so nobody can save it later.
2. **Optional team photo.** Turn on the highlight wall so the big screen rotates through opted-in notes, and shoot the group in front of it. Only opted-in notes ever appear there, so the photo is safe to share.
3. **End and delete the room.** Use the console's "End and delete" action and type the room code to confirm. This permanently deletes all notes and the roster. Exported images are untouched. Deleting on purpose beats waiting for the 24-hour sweep: it is your last act of keeping the anonymity promise.
